Exploring the Central and Western Heritage Trail

Man Mo Temple

Man Mo Temple was built in 1847 and now a Grade I historic building since 2009. It has been frequented by parents, students and scholars who worship the gods for their children or themselves to succeed in academia.

Tai Kwun

Tai Kwun was established to maintain law and order in Hong Kong from the 19th century onwards. It contains Central Police Station Compound, formal Central Magistracy and Victoria Prison Conpound.

Western Market

Western Market, renovated in 1991 and converted into a shopping complex, is an Edwardian-style building featuring shops selling arts and crafts, and fabric merchants who were originally located in the old alleys of Central.

Hong Kong Museums of Medical Sciences

Tai Ping Shan was the neighbourhood most severely impacted by the bubonic plague in 1894, which led to the establishment of the Bacteriological Institute in Hong Kong in 1906. The institute was relocated to a new site in 1972. The remaining Edwardian red brick style building, has been housing the Hong Kong Museum of Medical Sciences since 1995.

Dr Sun Yat-sen Museum

The Sheung Wan district was where Dr Sun Yat-sen was educated and baptized, and later planned for the revolution. Dr Sun Yat-sen Museum is a declared monument and a fascinating tourist attraction in Hong Kong, dedicated to a prominent figure of the same name, showcasing his various accomplishments and more.

YMCA

The centre of Chinese Young Men's Christian Association of Hong Kong (YMCA) is a six-storey blend of Chicago School architecture and Chinese roofing techniques. Completed in 1918, this building was greatly enriched when Western and Chinese influences came together during the previous era.

About the Central and Western Heritage Trail

The Antiquities and Monuments Office has set up a heritage trail in the Central and Western District that links together the historic buildings and sites in the vicinity with the aim of providing visitors with a better understanding of how this important area has grown and developed over the years. The entire heritage trail comprises three routes: the Central Route, the Sheung Wan Route, and the Western District and the Peak Route. All of them have received an enthusiastic response from the public. Although Hong Kong's urban landscape is constantly changing, many historic buildings still survive as witnesses to the growth and development of the city's districts.
